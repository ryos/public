Bike parks & tracks

Bend BMX01 — Race

21690 NE Neff Rd, Bend, OR 97701 · Practice + sanctioned USA BMX racing

One of the premier tracks in the Northwest, and the anchor for the whole trip. Sign-ups for practice and racing run in person or online through the track's own schedule page.

  • Weekday races run Mondays and Thursdays — practice/registration 5:00–6:00pm, race start 6:10pm.
  • Check the online schedule before you go — practice nights and race days vary week to week.
  • If a national or regional qualifier lands during your visit, expect a bigger crowd and a longer day (gates often open well before race time).
→ bendbmx.com
Big Sky Bike Park02 — Dirt Jumps

Adjacent to Bend BMX · Velosolutions pump track · jump lines · skills course · trials area · ~30 min needed

A Velosolutions-built asphalt pump track immediately adjacent to Bend BMX, alongside dirt jump lines, a skills area, and technical features — with a slopestyle terrain park and start tower planned for a future phase. It's a compact track, not a destination in itself: plan on about 30 minutes to sample it rather than a full session. Built with input from the local riding community, including former slopestyle pro Cam McCaul, who's been involved in advanced trail-building projects around Bend.

  • Helmets are required under 16 by Oregon law; pads recommended for everyone.
  • Restrooms are in the concession building near Field 6, south of the BMX track.
  • The skills course and singletrack were built with adaptive-rider access — expect to share features with adaptive riders.
→ Bend Park & Rec: Big Sky Bike Park

Mt. Bachelor Bike Park03 — Lift-Served

West Village base area, Mt. Bachelor · Lifts: Pine Marten Express · Sunshine Accelerator

More than a dozen miles of gravity trails, mostly reached off Pine Marten, with Little Pine as the beginner-friendly zone off Sunshine Accelerator. It's not a jump park on the scale of Whistler or Trestle — with one exception — but the trails matching your goals are well matched to a flow-and-jump-line day:

  • FTL — machine-built flow with wood, brick, and dirt berms plus placed jumps and rollers.
  • Lava Flow (upper/middle) — dirt berms, jumps, rollers, transitioning out of the lava fields.
  • Lower Lava Flow — reachable from mid/upper Lava Flow or off the Sunshine Accelerator top.
  • Rockfall — narrower and more technical; a good next step once FTL and Lower Lava Flow feel comfortable.
  • Redline — the flagship black-diamond jump trail, built by Kyle Jameson and Dirt Mechanics, often compared to Whistler's A-Line: step-up/step-down jumps, berms, rollers, and tabletops, tuned and watered daily. A strong match for Ray's BMX background — but high-consequence if ridden above ability, so size it up from the bottom before committing.

Tickets run as daily, a 3-day pack, or a season pass — buying the bike park pass by June 30 typically gets the lowest price, and the season pass includes two free days at sister parks (Mt. Hood, Whitefish MT, Schweitzer ID).

Cost-effective plan: bundling a lift ticket with a bike rental looks like the best value if you're not hauling your own downhill bikes up — no need to own or transport a dedicated park bike for one trip.

→ mtbachelor.com: bike park tickets → mtbachelor.com: bike rental + ticket packages
POV: Redline (full pull, 2025)
The Lair @ Phil's Trailhead04 — Dirt Jumps, Advanced

Phil's Trailhead, off Skyliners Rd, via Marvin's Garden · Progression-based freeride jump lines, maintained by COTA

Widely considered the undisputed premier dirt-jumping spot in Bend — a progression-based freeride area built with backhoes and volunteer labor, ranging from an "easily rollable" line up through a double-black, pro-only line for serious airtime. It's public land, built and maintained by the Central Oregon Trail Alliance (COTA) with USFS permission.

  • Jump lines are downhill-only; ride the climbing route (Marvin's Garden) to get back to the start.
  • Helmets required, body armor recommended — this is a genuine freeride area, not a beginner pump track.
  • Conditions vary — check bendtrails.org for recent rideability reports before heading out.
→ bendtrails.org: The Lair
POV: The Lair
Wanoga Sno-Park Trails05 — Downhill Singletrack

Cascade Lakes Hwy, ~14–15 miles SW of Bend · Key trails: Funner · Tiddlywinks · Tyler's Traverse · Storm King

A sno-park that doubles as the trailhead for one of Bend's best gravity-trail networks. Funner and Tiddlywinks are the classics — intermediate-to-advanced singletrack with banked turns, boulders, and jumps, best ridden as a climb-one/descend-the-other loop (about 13 miles round trip). COTA has also been adding a dedicated system of shorter, lappable downhill runs on the butte behind the parking lot, built with input from pro freeride riders including Cam Zink, Carson Storch, and Cam McCaul.

  • Both Funner and Tiddlywinks are built for descending — climbing either feels awkward; consider a shuttle (Cog Wild) if you want more laps and less climbing.
  • Rock sections have sharp lava rock — expect it to hurt more than dirt if you go down.
  • Good adaptive-rider access on several of the nearby trails (Royal Flush, Tiddlywinks).
→ Visit Bend: Wanoga downhill trails
